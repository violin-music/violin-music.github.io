## Musical Periods

Standard periods used in the catalog:

- **Renaissance** (c. 1400–1600)
- **Baroque** (c. 1600–1750)
- **Classical** (c. 1730–1820)
- **Romantic** (c. 1800–1910)
- **Late Romantic** (c. 1850–1920)
- **20th century** (c. 1900–2000)
- **Contemporary** (c. 1975–present)
- **Traditional** (pre-modern, varies by culture)

## Difficulty Rating Guide (Violin)

### ⭐ Beginner (1 star)
**Technical Requirements:**
- First position only
- Simple bow strokes (detaché, legato)
- Basic intonation patterns
- Limited string crossings
- Slow to moderate tempos

**Examples:** Happy Birthday, Deck the Halls, Muss I Denn

---

### ⭐⭐ Early Intermediate (2 stars)
**Technical Requirements:**
- First to third position
- Simple shifts between positions
- Basic vibrato (optional)
- String crossings at moderate tempo
- Simple ornaments
- Compound meters (6/8, 9/8)

**Examples:** Swallowtail Jig, Ashokan Farewell, Bear Dance

---

### ⭐⭐⭐ Intermediate (3 stars)
**Technical Requirements:**
- First through fifth position
- Controlled shifting
- Consistent vibrato
- Double stops (basic intervals)
- More complex rhythms and bowings
- Moderate to fast tempos
- Basic spiccato/staccato

**Examples:** Greensleeves, Bella ciao, Minor Swing, Gymnopédie

---

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Advanced (4 stars)
**Technical Requirements:**
- Full fingerboard facility (up to 7th position and beyond)
- Complex double stops and chords
- Advanced bowing techniques (spiccato, ricochet, col legno)
- Rapid passagework
- Fast string crossings
- Arpeggios across strings
- Advanced ornaments and slides

**Examples:** Brandenburg Concerto No. 3, Blue Lou, Daphné, Devil's Dream

---

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Professional (5 stars)
**Technical Requirements:**
- Virtuoso technique across entire fingerboard
- Complex multiple stops
- Harmonics (natural and artificial)
- Advanced left-hand pizzicato
- Extreme tempos
- Extended techniques
- Mature artistic interpretation

**Examples:** Badinerie, Passacaglia (Halvorsen), Scheherazade solo, Hora Mărțișorului
